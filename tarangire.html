Land of Giants

Tarangire National Park is famous for its massive elephant herds and ancient baobab trees that have stood for over 1,000 years. During dry season, the Tarangire River becomes the only water source for miles, attracting thousands of animals. With fewer tourists than Serengeti, you'll often have entire herds of 300+ elephants to yourself.

300+ Elephant Herds

Dry season Jun-Oct brings Tanzania's largest concentration of elephants. Herds of 300+ gather at Tarangire River daily.

Ancient Baobabs

Iconic baobab trees up to 30m tall and 2,000 years old. Some hollow trunks large enough to shelter elephants.

Tarangire River

Only permanent water source in 2,600 sq km. Wildlife migration corridor. Predator activity peaks here Jun-Oct.

550+ Bird Species

Best birding in Tanzania. Yellow-collared lovebirds, ashy starlings, and massive kori bustards year-round.

Diverse Landscapes

Granite ridges, river valleys, swamps, and acacia woodlands. More varied terrain than any other northern park.

No Crowds

Just 2 hours from Arusha but receives 10% of Serengeti's visitors. Perfect for exclusive safaris.

Experience the Giants

Best combined with Ngorongoro. Dry season books fast - secure your dates now.
